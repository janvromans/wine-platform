01 — Business Model

How Vinora makes money

Vinora operates as a marketplace intermediary — free for consumers, monetised through providers. Three complementary revenue streams create a resilient, scalable model.

🔗
Primary Revenue
Affiliate Commission
Earn a percentage of every sale that originates from a Vinora click-through. No sale, no cost to provider. Fully performance-based.
4–8% per transaction value · avg. bottle €45
⭐
Recurring Revenue
Provider Subscriptions
Stores and wineries pay a monthly fee for premium placement, enhanced profiles, analytics dashboards, and API access to list their inventory.
€49–€299/mo per provider · tiered plans
👑
Consumer Revenue
Vinora Premium
Wine lovers subscribe for price alerts, cellar management tools, exclusive pre-release allocations, and concierge sourcing for rare bottles.
€9.90/mo consumer subscription · freemium model

02 — Platform Features

What the platform does

Vinora is built in phases — starting with the core comparison engine and growing into a full wine lifestyle ecosystem. Here's the full feature vision.

🔍
Universal Wine Search
Phase 1 · Core

Real-time search across all connected providers. Search by name, region, grape, vintage, taste profile, food pairing, or price range.

Fuzzy search with typo tolerance
Natural language queries ("light Burgundy under €30")
Barcode scanner for in-store price check
Vintage chart integration
⚖️
Price Comparison Engine
Phase 1 · Core

Side-by-side comparison of every available listing including shipping, taxes, minimum orders, and total landed cost to your door.

Total cost calculator (incl. shipping)
Historical price charts per wine
Price alert notifications
Stock availability in real time
🚚
Logistics Optimizer
Phase 1 · Core

Compare shipping options across all providers. Filter by speed, cost, temperature control, and proximity to local pickup points.

Temperature-controlled courier network
Click & collect at partner stores
Multi-seller order consolidation
Real-time shipment tracking
🏠
My Cellar
Phase 2 · Premium

Digital cellar management — track your wine collection, drinking windows, valuations, and get notified when it's the perfect time to open a bottle.

Scan & add bottles from any purchase
AI drinking window predictions
Portfolio valuation tracker
Pair with meal planner

Food & wine pairing assistant
Gift finder with delivery

04 — Go-to-Market Strategy

How to launch and grow

Start in the Netherlands — a digitally mature, wine-loving market with strong logistics infrastructure. Then expand across the EU. Here's the full playbook.

01
Months 1–3 · Foundation
Build the Provider Network

Before launching to consumers, secure supply. Manually onboard 50 Dutch wine retailers, webshops, and a handful of direct wineries.

Cold outreach to top 50 NL wine webshops (free listing, no commitment)
Attend Prowein trade fair — pitch wineries directly
Partner with 1–2 logistics providers (PostNL, DHL Parcel)
Build data pipeline for first integrations
50
Providers onboarded
02
Months 4–6 · Launch
Consumer Beta Launch (NL)

Soft launch in the Netherlands. Focus on wine enthusiast communities, SEO, and earned media. No paid ads yet — validate product-market fit first.

Launch on Product Hunt + Dutch wine forums (Wijn.nl, Reddit r/wine)
SEO strategy: rank for "beste prijs [wine name]" queries
Wine blogger & influencer seeding (10–15 partnerships)
Email waitlist launch campaign
5K
Registered users
03
Months 7–12 · Scale
Scale in Benelux + Germany

With proven unit economics, start paid acquisition and expand the provider network into Belgium and Germany — both large wine e-commerce markets.

Google Shopping and branded search ads
Affiliate partnerships with wine magazines
Launch Vinora Premium subscription
Press outreach (FD, NRC, wine press)

Monthly active users

Year 2–3 · Expansion
Pan-European Platform

Expand to France, Spain, Italy, and the UK. Add B2B features for restaurants and wine bars. Raise a Series A to fund growth.
